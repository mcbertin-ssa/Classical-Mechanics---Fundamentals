We may always separate a system of particles, and form sub-systems, by using a definite criterium. For example, a system with \(n\in\mathbb{N}\) particles, each with mass \(m_1\), and another system with \(k\in\mathbb{N}\) particles, each with mass \(m_2\), may be seen as two sub-systems of a larger system with \(n+k\) particles with distinct masses. \footnote{Here, \(\mathbb{N}\) represents the set of natural numbers.}
